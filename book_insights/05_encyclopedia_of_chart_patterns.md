## Research Methodology

Purely empirical and data-driven — no gut feel, no anecdote.

- **75 distinct patterns** studied, including 23 previously undocumented ones
- **38,500+ chart pattern samples** in the 3rd Edition
- Performance measured at 1, 2, 3, and 6 months after the breakout
- Primary metric: **break-even failure rate** — % of patterns that fail to move at least 5% after breakout
- Each pattern assessed on: success rate, average gain, failure rate, ideal holding time, bull vs. bear market behaviour, volume shape

## Best Patterns for Your 1–7 Day Swing Trades

- Small patterns (up to 7 bars) have modest average gains (~7%) but are compact and fast
- For 1–7 day holds: **High and Tight Flag** and standard **Bull Flags** work best — compact, high-momentum, defined breakout
- **Price targets** (measured move) are most reliable over swing-trade horizons
- After a throwback, price resumes the original direction **86% of the time** within 10–11 days

**For your two setups:**
- Setup A (breakout from tight consolidation): Rectangle Bottom, High and Tight Flag, Ascending Triangle — top 3 performers
- Setup B (pullback to EMA): Maps to throwback entries — wait for throwback to complete (volume drying up, price stabilising), then enter the resumption

## Bull Flag — Two Very Different Animals

### High and Tight Flag (the only flag worth trading)
- **Criteria:** Stock must rise at least 90% in 2 months or less (near-vertical flagpole), then price moves sideways in tight horizontal consolidation
- **Success rate:** 85%
- **Break-even failure rate:** 0% in one study of 307 patterns — not one failed to move 5%
- Average gain after breakout: 39–69%

### Standard / Loose Bull Flag (avoid)
- If the flagpole is gradual or meandering: **fails 55% of the time**
- Average gain: only **9%** — barely above break-even

### Valid vs. Invalid Flag Identification

| Valid Flag | Invalid Flag |
|-----------|-------------|
| Sharp, near-vertical pole — 3+ large consecutive closes | Gradual, meandering price rise |
| Flag is tight, overlapping bars, horizontal to slightly downward | Flag drifts more than 50% back into the pole |
| Flag lasts under ~15 sessions | Flag lasts too long — becomes a downtrend |
| Volume contracts during flag | Volume expands during consolidation (distribution signal) |
| Volume expands on breakout above flag | Breakout on thin, below-average volume |

**Rule:** If you cannot identify a clear near-vertical pole in the preceding 2–4 weeks, skip the trade. The edge comes entirely from the momentum of the pole.

## Flat Base / Rectangle Pattern

- **Rectangle Bottom (upward breakout):** 85% success rate, 51% average gain
- **Rectangle Top (upward breakout):** 85% success rate, 51% average gain
- Price must fluctuate between clear horizontal support and resistance, at least 2–3 weeks, with minimum 2 clear touches of each boundary
- Slope between –0.1 and +0.1 (essentially flat): average gain 33% vs 24% for wider slopes
- Tighter the range = greater compression = more explosive breakout
- **Avoid downward breakouts from rectangles** — they perform poorly

## Ascending Triangle

- Upward breakouts occur **63% of the time** (direction is not guaranteed — do not assume)
- When upward: **83% success rate**, 43% average gain, 17% break-even failure rate
- Volume during formation: must be **declining** (consolidation, decreasing supply)
- Ideal volume shape: **falling then rising** heading into breakout
- At breakout: volume must **expand significantly** above the 30-day average
- Low breakout volume → higher probability of throwback

**Bulkowski's own warning:**
> "Ascending triangles often don't perform up to expectations — price rises for a few days, perhaps 5–10%, and then collapses. You have to be nimble for swing trading."

This is a short-swing pattern. Take your ₹2,000 target and exit. Don't hold for weeks expecting 43%.

## Volume — Exact Findings

1. **Rising volume trend in 3 weeks before breakout:** 31% fewer losers vs. patterns with falling pre-breakout volume

2. **High breakout-day volume:** Signals a stronger move BUT also increases throwback rate to **74%** — a high-volume breakout is more likely to pull back first before continuing

3. **Volume shapes (ranked by quality):**
   - **U-shape** (high at start, contracts mid-pattern, rises again near breakout): ideal for legitimate continuation
   - **Dome** (peaks mid-pattern): associated with distribution — treat with caution

4. **Core rule:** Volume must **contract during pattern** and **expand on breakout**. Expanding volume during consolidation + thin breakout volume = red flag for failure.

5. **Low-volume breakouts:** Smaller post-breakout moves but **lower throwback rates** — for 1–7 day holds, a moderate-volume breakout may serve better than an explosive one because it's less likely to pull back immediately

## Throwbacks and Pullbacks

**Throwback:** After an upward breakout, price returns to the breakout level within one month.

- Occur in **55%** of cases (sample: 26,542 patterns)
- High-volume breakout → throwback rate jumps to **74%**
- Average time to complete: **5 days** from breakout
- Average decline during throwback: **9%** (range 4–10%)
- **30%** of throwbacks continue below the pattern boundary

**What to do:**
- Do NOT panic-exit during a throwback — price resumes the original direction **86% of the time**
- Wait for volume to dry up at support + a bullish close before re-entering or adding
- If price breaks *below* the pattern (not just touches breakout level): re-evaluate — 30% failure scenario
- For your 1-week window: throwbacks typically complete within 5 days, so you may need to hold through it with stop below the pattern's support line

## Failed Breakouts — Warning Signs

**Failure rates in modern markets:**
- 1990s bull market: ~14% of breakouts fail to gain 10%
- 2003–2007 bull market: failure rate **doubled to 28%**
- Assume roughly **1 in 4 upward breakouts fails** — plan for it, don't be surprised by it

**Pre-failure signals:**
1. Narrow breakout — price barely clears resistance with no gap or surge
2. Below-average breakout volume — no conviction
3. Extended pattern — building for many months (information already priced in)
4. Prior trend already extended — stock ran 50–100% before the pattern
5. Market headwind — breakout against the broad market trend
6. **Busted pattern:** If price reverses and moves 10%+ in the opposite direction after a breakout, the pattern has "busted" — Bulkowski found busted patterns often produce powerful moves in the new direction

## Price Target: The Measured Move Formula

**Formula (works across all pattern types):**
```
Target = Breakout Price + Pattern Height

Pattern Height = Highest point in pattern − Lowest point in pattern
```

**Conservative version (recommended):**
```
Target = Breakout Price + (Pattern Height × % of patterns that reach full target)
```
Example: if only 65% of this pattern type reaches the full measured move, use 0.65 × height.

**Specific patterns:**
- **Ascending Triangle:** Height (horizontal top to lowest low) added to breakout
- **Rectangle:** Height of rectangle added to breakout
- **Flag:** Height of the flagpole added to the flag breakout point
- **Measured Move Up:** Second leg = first leg in price and time (works 83% of the time in bull market)

**Your gate:** If the measured move target is less than 2× your stop distance (less than ₹2,000 reward for ₹1,000 risk) → skip the trade. Pattern quality doesn't matter if the R:R doesn't qualify.
