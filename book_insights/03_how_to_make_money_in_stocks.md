## CANSLIM — Every Letter, Adapted for Indian Swing Trades

O'Neil studied every major stock winner from 1953 onward and found they all shared these 7 characteristics **before** their big moves.

---

### C — Current Quarterly Earnings
- Minimum: **+25% EPS growth YoY** vs. same quarter last year
- Preferred: 40–100%+ growth
- Acceleration is powerful: Q1 +20%, Q2 +35%, Q3 +50% = bullish signal
- Revenue must also grow — not just cost-cutting

**For your 1-week swings:** Use as a filter. Prefer stocks with a recent earnings beat or guidance raise. Don't require the full 25% — even positive earnings trend helps confirm the fundamental backdrop.

---

### A — Annual Earnings Growth
- 3-year EPS CAGR: minimum 25%
- Consistently rising EPS over 3 consecutive years
- ROE above 17%

**For your 1-week swings:** Skip this entirely. A 3-year EPS trend doesn't affect a 5-day price move.

---

### N — New Products, Services, Management, or Price Highs
- New product/service driving revenue
- New management (turnaround)
- New industry conditions
- **Stock hitting new 52-week highs** — O'Neil's most important "N" for technical traders

**Key counterintuitive insight:** Most traders fear buying stocks near all-time highs. O'Neil's research showed the opposite — stocks making new highs on strong fundamentals have already cleared all overhead resistance. They are the safest buys.

**For your swings:** New 52-week high or breakout from consolidation near all-time high = your "N" signal. That's all you need.

---

### S — Supply and Demand
- Prefer stocks with smaller float (fewer shares = bigger moves on institutional buying)
- Volume signature: **heavy up days, light down days** inside the base
- Stock buybacks = positive signal

**For NSE:** Prioritise stocks with minimum ₹5 crore daily turnover. Small-float NSE stocks with low liquidity will cause slippage. This overrides the float preference.

---

### L — Leader or Laggard
- Only buy market leaders: top 1–2 stocks in best-performing sector
- **RS Rating 80+ minimum, ideally 90+**
- Making new price highs while market is flat or rising
- NOT "cheap" recovering stocks

**The data behind this:** In O'Neil's study of 500 top performers, average RS Rating was **87 before the big move began**. You want proven momentum — not a bet on a loser's recovery.

**For NSE:** Compare 3-month stock return vs. Nifty 50. Stocks beating Nifty significantly = your leaders. TradingView has free RS Rating community scripts that work on NSE data.

---

### I — Institutional Sponsorship
- Rising institutional ownership in recent quarters
- At least 3–10 institutional investors holding the stock
- Not too much (&gt;80% institutional) — leaves no room for new buyers

**For NSE:** Use FII/DII net flow data (free on NSE website daily). Consistent FII buying days = institutional sponsorship. Avoid entries when FIIs are net sellers for 3+ consecutive days.

---

### M — Market Direction (Most Important — Check This First)
**3 out of 4 stocks follow the general market trend.**

O'Neil's direct quote: *"If you are reading this book during a bear market, do not expect the buy patterns to work. Most of them will definitely be defective. You absolutely do not buy breakouts during a bear market."*

Before looking at any individual stock, check:
1. Is Nifty 50 in an uptrend? (above 21-day and 50-day EMA?)
2. Distribution day count — less than 4 in the last 3 weeks?
3. Are FIIs net buyers recently?

If "No" to any: **wait. Do not take new long positions.**

## O'Neil's Chart Patterns — Exact Rules

### Flat Base (Best for Your 1-Week Swings — Tier 1)

**What it looks like:** After a 20–30%+ prior advance, stock moves nearly sideways in a tight, narrow range for weeks, then breaks out.

**Rules:**
- Prior uptrend: minimum 20%+ advance before the base
- Duration: minimum 5 weeks (7+ preferred)
- Depth: maximum **10–15% from peak to trough** — this shallowness is the defining feature
- Price action: stock holds near the top of its range, daily closes clustered tight
- Volume: **declining throughout the base**, then explosive spike on breakout

**Why it works for swing trades:** The shallow depth means the stock never really fell — institutions were simply holding. Breakouts from flat bases are often sharp and sustained, ideal for 3–7 day holds.

**Buy point:** 10 paisa above the highest point of the entire flat base.

---

### Cup with Handle (Tier 1 — Most Famous Pattern)

**What it looks like:** Strong advance → smooth "U" shaped rounding bottom (the cup) → small tight pullback in upper right (the handle) → breakout to new highs.

**Rules:**
- Prior uptrend: minimum 30%+ advance before the cup
- Cup duration: 7 weeks to 65 weeks (most reliable: 3–6 months)
- Cup depth: 12–33% from prior high (max 50% in volatile markets)
- Cup shape: smooth rounded "U", NOT a sharp "V"
- Handle: forms in the **upper half of the cup** (if lower half → weak pattern)
- Handle duration: 1–4 weeks
- Handle depth: max 10–15% drop, slope drifts slightly down on light volume

**Volume during handle:** must **dry up completely** — lowest in the entire base. Then volume surges 40–50%+ on breakout day.

**Buy point:** 10 paisa above the highest point of the handle.

---

### Double Bottom (Tier 2)

**What it looks like:** The letter "W" — falls, bounces, falls again to roughly the same level, bounces and breaks above the middle of the W.

**Rules:**
- Duration: minimum 7 weeks
- Depth: less than 40% from prior high
- **Critical rule:** the second bottom must slightly undercut the first bottom (shakes out weak holders)
- Second bottom volume: lower than first bottom (selling exhausting)

**Buy point:** 10 paisa above the **middle peak of the W** — NOT at the second bottom.

---

### High Tight Flag (Rarest, Most Powerful)

**Rules:**
- Flagpole: stock must **double (100%+ rise) in 8 weeks or less** — mandatory requirement
- Flag duration: 3–5 weeks
- Flag depth: max 10–25% correction
- Flag appearance: tight, orderly, low-volatility

This is Bulkowski's top-ranked pattern (85% success rate). See also the Bulkowski insights file.

**For NSE:** Very rare. When it appears (usually small-cap momentum stocks after a major catalyst), size conservatively.

## Volume Rules — Exact

1. **Breakout volume minimum:** at least **40–50% above the 50-day average daily volume** — no exceptions
2. **No volume = no breakout:** price above buy point on average volume = false breakout, do not buy
3. **Inside the base:** heavy up weeks, light down weeks = institutional accumulation. Reverse = distribution = avoid.
4. **Handle volume dry-up:** lowest volume in the entire base during the handle = sellers gone, ready to move
5. **NSE-specific:** check delivery volume % — above 50% delivery on breakout day confirms institutional buying

## Relative Strength — O'Neil's Definition

O'Neil's RS Rating is NOT the RSI indicator — it is a **price performance percentile vs. all other stocks** over 52 weeks, weighted toward the most recent quarter.

**Minimum to consider:** RS 80 (stock outperformed 80% of all stocks)
**Ideal:** RS 90+

**RS line making new highs simultaneously with price breakout** = the strongest confirmation possible.

**For NSE (since IBD RS isn't available for Indian stocks):**
- Compare stock's 3-month % return vs Nifty 50's 3-month return
- Use free community RS Rating scripts on TradingView (search "IBD RS Rating NSE")
- Chartink has relative performance filters

## The Pivot Point (Buy Point) — Exact Rules

**Where to buy:**
- **10 paisa above the highest point of the pattern structure** (handle high, flat base high, etc.)

**The 5% buy zone:**
- Valid entry: from pivot (₹X + 0.10) to pivot × 1.05 (5% above)
- Above 5% from pivot = "extended" → do not buy, wait for next setup

**Critical rules:**
- Never buy early (before the pivot is cleared)
- Never buy more than 5% above the pivot
- Volume must confirm — breakout on quiet volume = trap
- Add-on buy: if initial buy works and stock rises 2–3%, can add a smaller second position

## Sell Rules — Defense and Offense

### Defensive (Loss Control)

**The 7–8% stop rule:**
> "Cut every single loss when it is 7% or 8% below your purchase price, with absolutely no exception."

- Trigger: stock falls 7–8% below YOUR purchase price
- Action: sell immediately at market, no limit orders, no waiting for a bounce
- No exceptions: not for "great company," not for "it'll come back"

**For your account:** Your stop is sized by ₹1,000 risk, not by percentage. A 7–8% stop is the absolute cap — your actual stop should be a tight technical level (usually 3–5% for swing setups) that keeps you within the ₹1,000 limit.

**If a stock drops back below the pivot on heavy volume after a breakout:** exit immediately — the base has failed.

### Offensive (Profit Taking)

**The 20–25% profit rule:**
- Most stocks make their first significant run 20–25% from a proper pivot breakout, then pause
- Take profits at **20–25% gain** unless the stock shows exceptional behaviour

**Climax top signal — sell into strength:**
- Stock suddenly accelerates upward at a steep angle
- On the heaviest volume in weeks
- Daily range expands dramatically, price moves 20%+ in a week after a long run
- This is often the final top → sell into this, don't wait for reversal

**For your 1-week hold:** Your ₹2,000 target (2× risk) is your profit-taking rule. Take it when it's there. The 20–25% long-term target doesn't apply to your timeframe.

## Market Timing — O'Neil's Full System

### Follow-Through Day (FTD) — Confirming a New Bull Phase

**Definition:** After a market correction, once an attempted rally begins:
- Day 1 of rally = first up close
- Must wait for **Day 4 or later** of the attempted rally
- On FTD: major index closes up significantly (1%+ minimum) on **higher volume** than the previous day
- After FTD: begin carefully buying leading stocks at proper buy points

**FTD timing data:**
- Days 1–3: fail nearly every time
- Days 4–7: most reliable

### Distribution Days — Market Top Warning

**Definition:** Major index falls 0.2%+ on volume higher than the previous day.

**Count rules:**
- 4–5 distribution days in 3–4 weeks: market under pressure → reduce buying
- 6+ distribution days: go defensive → stop new positions, trim existing ones
- Distribution days expire after 25 trading days

**For NSE:** Track Nifty 50 daily. Count distribution days manually using TradingView. Also track FII net flow — consecutive net selling days align with distribution days.

## Honest Critiques — What Doesn't Work on NSE

1. **Survivorship bias** — O'Neil only shows patterns that worked. Real success rates are lower than implied.

2. **CANSLIM funds underperformed in real life** — the CANGX CANSLIM ETF trailed the S&P 500 and was shut down in 2021. Theory and practice diverge.

3. **IBD ecosystem lock-in** — IBD RS ratings, MarketSmith tools are US-focused. MarketSmith India exists but costs ₹1,500–3,000/month — significant overhead on a ₹1L account. Use Screener.in and TradingView instead.

4. **Quarterly earnings calendar** — Indian companies report results with irregular timing. Check NSE corporate calendar before every entry. Avoid stocks reporting earnings within 3 weeks of your entry.

5. **FII/DII flow distorts patterns** — sudden FII block sells can break valid technical setups within 2 days. Always check daily FII/DII net flow before entering.

6. **Liquidity on NSE mid-caps** — many NSE stocks have low volume. A "50% above average volume" breakout may still be only 5,000 shares — too thin for institutional backing. Minimum ₹5 crore daily turnover before trusting a breakout.

7. **Stage counting matters** — first and second stage bases are far more reliable than 3rd/4th stage. By stage 3+, most of the big move is done. Avoid late-stage breakouts.
