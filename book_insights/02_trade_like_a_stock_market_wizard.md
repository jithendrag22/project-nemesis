## SEPA — The Four Components (All Must Align)

SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis) is NOT just a chart pattern — it is a four-component filter where all four must align simultaneously.

| Component | What It Means |
|-----------|-------------|
| **S — Specific Entry Point** | Exact price where stock breaks out of VCP on expanding volume — the pivot |
| **E — Earnings** | Quarterly EPS growth ≥ 20% YoY, accelerating, plus rising annual trend |
| **P — Price Action** | VCP forming inside a confirmed Stage 2 uptrend; MAs in correct order |
| **A — Announcement/Catalyst** | Specific fundamental driver: earnings surprise, new product, institutional buying increasing |

**Core rule:** Perfect chart + poor fundamentals = fail. Strong earnings + broken chart = fail. All four must line up.

## VCP — Volatility Contraction Pattern (The Only Setup Minervini Uses)

### Exact Definition
A consolidation pattern where price range AND volume **progressively contract** through a series of smaller pullbacks. Sellers exhaust their supply while buyers quietly accumulate. When supply dries up, even modest buying pressure produces a sharp upward move.

### The 7-Point VCP Checklist

1. **Progressive contractions** — Each pullback measurably smaller than the previous. Textbook: 25% → 12% → 6%. Each should be 20–30% smaller in range than the one before. Minimum 2 contractions; ideal is 3–6.

2. **Volume dry-up** — Selling volume decreases with each successive pullback. Final contraction should have the lowest volume in the entire base.

3. **Clear pivot point** — Near the end, price settles into an extremely tight range. The pivot = high of the final, tightest contraction. This is the exact buy trigger.

4. **Stage 2 uptrend confirmed** — Price above 50, 150, and 200-day SMAs, all in proper alignment.

5. **Relative Strength** — RS above 70 minimum, ideally 80–90+.

6. **Earnings criteria** — 20%+ quarterly EPS growth with acceleration.

7. **Market environment** — 90%+ of successful breakouts occur in confirmed bull markets.

### VCP Stages

| Stage | Description |
|-------|------------|
| Prior uptrend | Stock already advanced 30–100%+ — stores energy |
| Contraction 1 | Widest, deepest pullback (15–35%) |
| Contraction 2 | Shallower than #1 (8–18%) |
| Contraction 3 | Tight (3–8%), volume very low |
| Final pivot | Price nearly motionless, lowest volume in base |
| Breakout | Clears pivot on 40–50%+ above average volume |

### What a VCP Is NOT
- Not a random sideways channel
- Not valid without progressive shrinkage in both price range AND volume
- A 60%+ correction is not a VCP — too much overhead supply

## Entry Rules

**Rule 1 — The Pivot:** Entry is the high of the final, tightest contraction. Place a buy-stop-limit just above: if pivot is ₹500, enter at ₹501–₹503.

**Rule 2 — The Buy Zone:** Entry must be within 0–5% of the pivot. If the stock has already moved 5%+ above the pivot when you notice it, skip and wait for the next setup.

**Rule 3 — Volume (Non-Negotiable):** Breakout volume must be at least 40–50% above the 50-day average. Low or average volume breakout = suspect, likely a false breakout.

**Rule 4 — Market must be in uptrend:** Nifty 50 above its key MAs. If market is breaking down, even perfect VCPs fail.

**Rule 5 — Never buy extended:** More than 5% above pivot = do not chase.

**The Squat (False Breakout):** Price breaks above the pivot but closes back below it the same day or next. If this happens → exit immediately. Do not hold and hope.

---

## Stop-Loss Rules

**Rule 1:** Stop is placed **below the low of the final, tightest contraction** in the VCP — just below where supply was exhausted. If price returns there, the setup has failed.

**Rule 2 — Maximum stop distance:** Absolute maximum 7–8% below entry. Average in practice: 4–5%. If the required stop is more than 8% away, pass — wait for the setup to tighten more.

**Rule 3 — Never widen a stop.** Once set, the stop only moves UP (trailing in your favour), never down. Moving it lower turns a controlled loss into an account-damaging loss.

**Rule 4 — Closing-price stops:** A stock must close below the stop level to trigger exit (intraday dips are acceptable if they recover). Exception: sharp intraday breakdown on heavy volume → exit without waiting for close.

## Exit Rules

### Cutting Losers
1. Stock hits your stop → exit immediately, no debate
2. Average loss should be 4–5%; absolute maximum 10%
3. Trade goes red on day of entry and closes significantly below pivot → exit
4. Squat (false breakout) → exit immediately
5. Never hold and hope: if the stock acts wrong (breaks down on heavy volume), cut it

### Taking Profits
- Begin taking partial profits at 20–25% gain (standard holds)
- At that point, sell at least 50% of the position
- Trail the remainder using the 21-day EMA as a trailing stop
- For stronger stocks: use 50-day SMA as the trailing stop
- Full exit: close below 50-day SMA on above-average volume, or earnings decelerate sharply

### For Your 1-Week Hold
- Exit at your 1:2 R:R target (₹2,000 profit) OR on Day 5, whichever comes first
- If stock doesn't move +2% in 3–4 days → time-stop exit (dead money)
- Do not hold beyond your plan just because "it looks good"

## Honest Critiques

1. **Survivorship bias** — book examples only show patterns that worked. Track every setup you take (winners AND losers) to build your real-world success rate.

2. **Works primarily in bull markets** — in sideways markets, even perfect VCPs fail at high rates. Build a strict market environment filter. In bear markets, 100% cash is the right trade.

3. **Indian market: fewer superperformers** — NSE has ~200–300 liquid mid-caps worth trading vs. thousands in US. In low-setup environments, wait. Quality over quantity.

4. **Fundamentals are harder to source in real time in India** — use Screener.in for quarterly earnings data. Focus on stocks that recently reported strong results and are now forming VCPs.

5. **False breakouts are more common than implied** — especially in choppy markets. Accept that multiple small −1R losses in a row is normal. Exit squats immediately, re-assess environment, move on.

6. **Pattern recognition is subjective** — use objective measurement: is each pullback measurably smaller in % than the previous? If you can't answer "yes" definitively, it's not a valid VCP.

7. **Crypto application** — crypto has no earnings (E component). Use only the technical components: Trend Template, VCP, volume, pivot. For BTC/ETH, this is sufficient.
