# Project Nemesis — Books & Learning Resources

## Priority Reading Order (start here, in this sequence)

---

### 1. Trading in the Zone — Mark Douglas
**Read this first. Before charts. Before anything.**

This is not a technical book. It is about why traders lose even when they have a good strategy.
Covers: discipline, handling losses without revenge, thinking in probabilities, detaching from individual trade outcomes.

The reason 90% of traders fail is not bad strategies — it is psychology. This book fixes that.

---

### 2. Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard — Mark Minervini
**The closest book to what Project Nemesis is trying to do.**

Minervini is a US Investing Champion who built his entire edge around:
- Tight consolidation breakouts (exactly Setup A in your rules)
- Strict stop losses (never give a trade more than 7-8% room)
- Position sizing based on risk, not conviction
- Scanning for stocks near 52-week highs with strong relative strength

His SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis) methodology maps almost 1:1 to your strategy.
After reading this, your Setup A rules will make complete sense intuitively.

---

### 3. How to Make Money in Stocks — William O'Neil
**For stock selection fundamentals.**

O'Neil created the CANSLIM system — a way to identify stocks that are about to make big moves
by combining earnings growth + price/volume action. Even if you skip the fundamental parts,
Part 3 (chart patterns) is essential — it covers cups, handles, flat bases, all of which
produce the tight consolidations your Setup A looks for.

Teaches you: how to read a chart pattern, how volume confirms a move, what a proper base looks like.

---

### 4. Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom — Van Tharp
**For position sizing and expectancy.**

This book introduces the concept of "R" (risk multiples) — the same 1R, 2R language in your strategy.
Tharp shows mathematically why expectancy (your win rate × average winner, minus loss rate × average loser)
is what drives account growth — not picking the "right" stocks.

Your target: 60% × 2R – 40% × 1R = +0.8R per trade. This book explains why that is powerful.

---

### 5. The Encyclopedia of Chart Patterns — Thomas Bulkowski
**Reference, not cover-to-cover.**

Bulkowski backtested hundreds of chart patterns and published success rates for each.
Use it as a lookup: "I'm seeing a bullish flag on this stock — what is the historical breakout rate?"
Gives you data-backed confidence in your setups rather than guessing.

High-probability patterns for your style: Bull Flag, Flat Base, Ascending Triangle, High Tight Flag.

---

### 6. Reminiscences of a Stock Operator — Edwin Lefèvre
**Read when you have a few weeks of trading behind you.**

Semi-fictional biography of Jesse Livermore, one of the greatest traders ever.
The lessons are timeless: pyramiding into winning trades, cutting losers fast,
not fighting the tape, understanding market cycles.

Light read, story format, but every chapter has a principle you'll recognize from your own trades.

## What NOT to watch/read yet

- Options strategies (wait until you have 6 months of profitable swing trading)
- F&O leverage trades (your capital is too small and the risk is too high right now)
- Stock tips from Telegram groups, YouTube "analysts", or Twitter/X traders
- Day trading courses — your strategy is not intraday, the skills are different

## Learning Sequence (3-month plan)

| Month | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| Month 1 | Read Douglas + Minervini. Paper trade 10 setups using your rules. No real money. |
| Month 2 | Start with ₹25,000 of your ₹1L. 2–3 trades. Build execution habit. |
| Month 3 | Scale to ₹50,000. Refine based on your journal. Read O'Neil + Van Tharp. |
| Month 4+ | Full ₹1L capital. 4–8 trades per month at ₹1,000 risk each. Review weekly. |
